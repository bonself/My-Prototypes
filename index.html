Prototype II: Marsupial Robotic Experiment System


The Prototype of Mother Robot
A Prototype of the Children Robots
As shown in the above pictures, there were the mother Robot and a child robot invovled in the marsupial robotic system that I designed for my PHD Project. I designed all the system and machine of them and developed several docking approaches for the mother robot to receive three children robots. The first approach is based on the inspection and guidance based on the machine vision of the mother robot. The second is based on the embedded vision and decision of the child robot. The last and the most efficient one is via the coordination visual servo between them. The most striking feature of the mother robot is its docking station equiped with a mechanism that rises up or descends down via lead-screw. It could receive three children robots at most. The child robot was developed based on embedded system and I desigend its printed-circuit boards(PCB).

Prototype III: Mini Mobile Manipulator




  
The two major components of the mini mobile manipulator
The mobile manipulator illustrated in the above picture consists of a wheeled mobile platform and a mini-manipulator. I designed the two components with nomalacrylic plate and several second-hand motors, as made them easy to be producted and cheap. The end of the manipulator was equiped with a rubber suction nozzle driven by a micro-vacuum pump to pcik things up, while a CMOS camera was mounted beside the nozzle for alignment servo. The manipulator also palyed the role of child robot in my marsupial robotic system and it was developed based on an embedded system as shown in the following picture.

Prototype IV: -Low Cost Guiding Robot Based on Embedded System.





  
The low cost guiding robot and the introducing picture played on its screen
Based on the technical experience of developing embedded systems and mobile robots, I began to create low cost indoor service robot after getting my PHD degree. Now I am leading two other freshman team to do this. I designed the embedded control system, schematic circuit schematic diagram and a freshman turned them into PCB boards. And the other freshman helps me debugging different devices of the robots. I also finished the programming by myself. The low cost guiding robot shown in the pictures above was developed in this way. The robot can calculated its indoor position coordinates based on landmarks，onboard IMU and the motor encoders. It can guiding the people to the demanded room and its manufacturing cost is less than 550 US dollars. The following pictures show its embedded control system.
